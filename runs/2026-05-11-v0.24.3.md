## Real findings closed in v0.24.3

### Polish #1 -- legacy env var warning

The reviewer's valid concern: a user with the pre-v0.24.2
`OPEN_CODE_MANAGED_SETTINGS=...` in their `.bashrc` silently gets
production defaults after the rename, with no indication.

Fix: detect the legacy name and emit a one-line stderr notice:
```
[managed-settings: OPEN_CODE_MANAGED_SETTINGS is no longer honored
(security: rename caused by v0.24.2). Use
OPEN_CODE_MANAGED_SETTINGS_TEST for tests, or move your
managed.json to the conventional location.]
```

Probe (`probe_managed_settings.py` test 6): sets the legacy var,
loads settings, asserts (a) the legacy file's `model` does NOT
override the project's, AND (b) the stderr warning fires.

### Polish #2 -- post-compact seq regression guard

Even though the reviewer's claim was wrong, the assertion is
valuable: "seq numbers stay monotonic + contiguous across a compact
event" is the kind of invariant that someone could break in a
future refactor without noticing.

Probe (`probe_session_perf.py` test 5): writes 5 messages, a
compact event, then 2 more messages. Asserts the file's msg events
have seq `[0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6]` (no gap, no duplicate).

## Items intentionally NOT addressed

The reviewer flagged two additional polish items that I'm deferring:

1. **`s=session` sticky still tool-name granular** (open_code.py:1077)
   -- reviewer noted this is pre-existing, not a v0.24.x regression.
   The asymmetry with `a=always` (which v0.24.1 made arg-scoped) is a
   real design tension but changing it would require a UX rethink
   (e.g. "s=session-this-args / S=session-all-args" or a follow-up
   prompt). Out of scope for a hot-fix release.

2. **Subagent transcript cache priming** (subagents.py:184)
   -- `open_subagent_transcript` doesn't call `store.create()` so
   `_msg_counts` isn't primed for subagent sessions. The cold-scan
   on first append still fires (correct), just missing the O(1)
   optimization. Edit-cost vs benefit: subagent sessions are
   short-lived (<= default 8 turns), so the optimization wouldn't
   move the needle. Deferred.

## Reverted change

I initially over-applied the reviewer's claim and added a
`self._msg_counts.pop(session.id, None)` call inside `append_compact`.
After running a trace, it became clear the pop did NOTHING -- both
with and without it, post-compact behavior is identical. I reverted
the pop and added a docstring explaining why it ISN'T needed.

This is the "trust but verify" pattern in action: the reviewer's
specific suggested fix was wrong, but adding a regression guard
that documents the correct behavior was still valuable.
